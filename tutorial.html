I'll be producing a step by step Gimp tutorial to help you along in your xraying needs.

Key points to remember are:

  • Big size is win
  • Good lighting is win (no under-exposed or over-exposed pics)
  • Light clothing works best
  • Bra under clothing is bad
  • Instagram filters makes things dificult

There are many more points to consider, but these are the important ones.

Time for the tutorial!

1.

First thing you want to do is find a suitable pic that you want to x-ray. I have selected this one:

2.

Then go in to "Quick Select" by pressing Shift-Q or going to Select and "Toggle Quick Select", and then use the Paint Brush in the Toolbox painting white to select the area you want to x-ray. Press Shift-Q again to exit "Quick Select"

3.

Copy the selected area, and paste it in as a new layer. Then drag the area to the correct position.

4.

Open the Channels dialog in the Layers - Brushes window, and deselect and turn off two of the colors, but leave Alpha on. Go to Colors, and enter the Levels adjustment.

5.

This is where the magic happens. Adjust the sliders to bring out as much detail as you can, while still maintaining resolution. Good rule of thumb is to keep the two sliders on the ends at each end of the "mountain" and slide the middle one a bit around to get brigness. Idealy you want to get each color to come as close to the skin-color as possible, as that will make it easier later on.

6.

When you have done this to all three colors you probably need to adjust the hue. So enter the Colors - Color Balance with all three color-channels selected, and move the sliders gently around to match skintone. This is done on Shadows, Midtone and Highlights. Go back and forth on all three to ajust as close as possible. It helps to occasionally click OK and re-enter in to Color Balance to adjust more closely.

7.

Some final adjustments are in order, and now we bring out the Dodge/Burn tool. With this we can darker the needed areas (some shadows, and also the areola around the nipple needs to darken) and also lighten to bring out more shape. This step takes some practice to get the hang of. One good tip is to use very low Exposure on the tool and a very soft brush. That way you won't get too many hard edges.

8.

And there you are. A completed x-ray.
